From Kigali we serve commercial fleets on both the Northern Corridor (to Mombasa via Kampala) and the Central Corridor (to Dar es Salaam), plus urban 2W/3W fleets within the city.

NAIROBI
HUB

East Africa's commercial capital. The Nairobi Hub anchors the Northern Corridor and serves Kenya's rapidly growing EV fleet market.

NAIROBI HUB
Nairobi, Kenya
Hub Overview

Nairobi is East Africa's economic gateway. With the Port of Mombasa 480km away and direct connections to Uganda, Rwanda and beyond, the Nairobi Hub sits at the crossroads of the continent's busiest freight corridors.

Kenya's commercial fleet operators face dramatic fuel cost pressures. Kabisa Nairobi provides the sales, service and charging infrastructure to make the switch to electric commercially viable.

Data Sources

This page combines observed market data where available, and modeled estimates where direct data is limited. Country analysis draws on vehicle parc estimates, annual new vehicle sales (AAAM, NAAMSA, local transport ministries), EV penetration rates, and fuel consumption assumptions from IEA and World Bank transport datasets.

Model Assumptions

·Freight: 80,000 km/yr avg · 28L/100km diesel
·Consumer: 18,000 km/yr avg · 8L/100km petrol
·EV: 1.8 kWh/km (freight) · 0.18 kWh/km (consumer)
