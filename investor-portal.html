Investment Framework
Understanding the Two Investment Vehicles
Kabisa offers two distinct paths to invest. The table below compares them side by side to help you choose the right vehicle for your portfolio strategy.
TopCo Co-Investment
Equity · SAFE · Broad Platform
What you own
Equity in Kabisa TopCo — the ultimate parent of all Hubs and Corridors entities across every market.
Instrument
SAFE note — converts at 20% discount to next priced round (Series A). No maturity, no interest.
Returns profile
Platform equity upside on full exit or IPO. Higher risk, higher potential return. No fixed coupon.
Best suited for
VC funds, angel investors, and growth equity investors comfortable with early-stage equity risk.
Minimum
$250,000 per investor
Corridors & SPV Investment
Debt or Equity · Asset-Backed · Country SPVs
What you own
Debt or equity stake in a country SPV (Rwanda, Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda) — each held under Kabisa Corridors Ltd.
Instrument
Senior secured debt (fixed coupon, asset-backed) or SPV equity. Debt is senior to equity in repayment.
Returns profile
Debt investors receive a fixed coupon backed by infrastructure assets. Equity investors share in country-level upside.
Best suited for
Infrastructure funds, DFIs, family offices, and impact investors seeking asset-backed exposure.
Minimum
$50,000 debt / $25,000 equity
Corporate Structure
Investment Architecture
Two parallel Mauritius holding companies — Kabisa Corridors Ltd for infrastructure SPVs, and Kabisa Hubs Ltd for city OpCos. Investors participate proportionally through either vehicle.

Overview & Company
What is Kabisa? +
Kabisa is East Africa's leading EV charging infrastructure company, building the backbone for zero-emission freight and mobility. We operate charging hubs and corridor stations across Rwanda, Kenya, Tanzania and Uganda, with expansion into Ethiopia and DRC underway.
What stage is Kabisa at? +
Kabisa is post-traction and pre-Series A. We have 5 operational stations in Rwanda, 2,000+ EVs on our network, 300+ EVs sold, and $51K/month in charging revenue as of December 2025. We've completed 12 full EV truck corridor trips across borders.
What problem does Kabisa solve? +
East Africa has rapidly growing EV adoption (led by Rwanda's government mandate) but zero cross-border charging infrastructure. Diesel trucks dominate freight. Kabisa replaces that with a network of fast-chargers along the continent's most important trade corridors.
Who are Kabisa's customers? +
150+ commercial customers including the US Embassy, international NGOs, logistics operators and corporate fleets. Revenue comes from charging sessions (pay-per-kWh), EV sales commissions, fleet service contracts, and B2B charging subscriptions.
Investment Structure
What are the two ways to invest? +
Path 1 — TopCo SAFE: Invest into Kabisa TopCo via a SAFE with 20% discount to Series A. Broad platform exposure across all Hubs and Corridors markets. Min. $250K.

Path 2 — Corridors SPV: Invest directly into a country SPV (Rwanda, Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda) held under Kabisa Corridors Ltd. Structured as senior secured debt or equity, with capital deployed into charging infrastructure.
Where is Kabisa incorporated? +
Kabisa has a two-tier Mauritius holding structure. TopCo sits above two parallel vehicles: Kabisa Corridors Ltd (corridor SPVs) and Kabisa Hubs Ltd (city OpCos). Standard African infrastructure investment structure.
Is there a minimum investment? +
TopCo SAFE minimum is $250,000. For SPVs: $50,000 for debt tranches, $25,000 for equity. Contact us to discuss your preferred structure.
Do I need to sign an NDA? +
As a credentialed investor with portal access you already have access to the Data Room. Full term sheets, financial models and legal documents are available in the Data Room tab. An NDA may be required before final term sheet execution.
TopCo / SAFE
How does the SAFE work? +
A SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) converts to equity at a 20% discount to Kabisa's next priced round. No maturity date, no interest. Converts upon a priced equity round, liquidity event, or dissolution. Full terms in the Data Room.
What is the valuation cap? +
The full SAFE terms including valuation cap are in the Data Room. Open the Data Room tab to access the term sheet directly.
How much has been committed? +
$1.2M committed against a $6M target as of March 2026. The round is open.
Corridors & SPV Investment
What is an SPV investment? +
Each country (Rwanda, Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda) has a dedicated SPV holding charging infrastructure assets. Investors participate as debt holders (senior, secured) or equity holders within a given country SPV.
How does capital flow into the SPV? +
Capital invested into a country SPV is deployed directly into physical charging infrastructure — station equipment, grid connections, land leases, and installation. Kabisa Corridors Ltd acts as the holding company above each country SPV. Full deployment details and use-of-proceeds breakdowns are in the Data Room.
Which country SPVs are raising? +
Rwanda SPV — $2M raise ($1M debt fully committed, $200K equity committed). Active revenue.

Tanzania SPV — $2.5M raise ($1M debt committed). 7 stations, scaling.

Kenya SPV — $2M indicative. Term sheet in structuring.

Uganda SPV — $2M early stage. $500K debt committed.
Is debt investment asset-backed? +
Yes. Debt investments are secured against physical charging infrastructure assets within each SPV. Full security terms, priority waterfall and collateral details are in the Data Room.
Returns & Financials
What returns can I expect? +
Full financial projections are in the Data Room. Rwanda is the most mature market: $51K/month growing at 11.4× year-on-year. Multi-market profitability projected by 2027 as corridor stations build out. Debt instruments have negotiated fixed returns; equity is subject to business performance.
What is Kabisa's revenue model? +
Four streams: (1) Pay-per-kWh charging fees, (2) EV sales commissions through Hub partnerships, (3) Fleet service and maintenance contracts, (4) B2B charging subscriptions for corporate fleets.
Has Kabisa been audited? +
Audited accounts for Rwanda OpCo are in the Data Room. Tanzania and Uganda entities are being audited for FY2025. Management accounts are available for all entities.
Operations
How many stations are operational? +
5 active stations in Rwanda (including East Africa's first 240kW fast charger), 2 active stations in Kenya (mobile battery), and 4 active stations in Tanzania (Dar, Dodoma, Singida, Kahama) and 1 in Ethiopia. 12+ stations under development or with lease agreements in place.
What corridors have been tested? +
(1) Dar–Kigali (Central) — 12 full EV truck trips, 1,400km. (2) Kigali–Mombasa (Northern) — 1,850km tested. (3) Addis–Nairobi — 1,300km tested. All corridors anchor in Kigali.
What EV brands does Kabisa support? +
Multi-standard stations supporting CCS2, CHAdeMO, and Type 2 connectors — covering BYD, JAC, King Long, and other major brands operating in East Africa.
How to Invest
What is the investment process? +
1. Review materials — term sheets, financial model, legal structure in Data Room.
2. Intro call — 30-min call with Kabisa's investment team.
3. Term sheet — agree terms for your chosen path.
4. Legal & KYC — standard AML/KYC via Mauritius legal counsel.
5. Wire & close — funds transfer and documentation.
How long does the process take? +
From first review to wire, most investors close in 3–6 weeks.
Who do I contact to start? +
Email invest@kabisa.africa with "Investor Inquiry" in the subject. Our team responds within 24 hours. Or go directly to the Data Room tab to review all documents.
